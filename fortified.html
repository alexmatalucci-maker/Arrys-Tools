The Three Upgrades

Where Roofs Fail —
And What FORTIFIED Fixes

Storm damage almost always begins in the same three places. FORTIFIED targets each one with a tested requirement, not a guess.

2×
Uplift resistance gained by switching to 8D ring-shank nails in a tighter pattern — per IBHS testing.
95%
Reduction in water intrusion once the seams of the roof deck are properly sealed.
01
Enhanced Deck Attachment
Ring-shank nails instead of common smooth nails, spaced closer together. This is what keeps the deck bolted to your house when pressure builds.
02
Sealed Roof Deck
If wind strips the covering off, bare plywood is exposed. IBHS measured it: every inch of rain on an open deck can dump the equivalent of nine bathtubs of water into a home. Sealing the seams stops that.
03
Locked-Down Roof Edges
Damage usually starts when wind gets under the edge and peels. FORTIFIED requires a wider drip edge plus a fully adhered starter strip working as one system.

How It Gets Verified

It’s Not What We Say.
It’s What Gets Documented.

Any contractor can call a roof “hurricane ready.” A FORTIFIED Roof is photographed at every required stage, then reviewed by someone who doesn’t work for us.

Arry’s Installs to the Standard
Our crews build to the FORTIFIED Roof requirements — deck attachment, sealed deck, locked edges, rated covering.

03

The Wind Mitigation Form Changed

For the first time in over 12 years, Florida’s wind mitigation inspection form was updated — and it now includes a section that specifically recognizes FORTIFIED designation. Your roof can finally be credited for how it was actually built.

20–40%
Range some local homeowners have reported on the wind portion of their premium.

That figure comes from what we’re seeing locally and from the program representative. It applies to the wind portion — not necessarily your entire premium. Every carrier and policy is different, so always confirm directly with your agent.

Staying Ahead of Roof Age

Carriers are scrutinizing roof age harder than ever in this market. Maintaining the designation every five years helps demonstrate your roof’s condition and continued compliance as it ages — which can be valuable when future insurance requirements come up.

Insurance outcomes are determined solely by your carrier. Arry’s Roofing does not guarantee premium reductions or coverage decisions. Savings examples reflect local homeowner reports and program representatives — not a quote.
