Common questions about solar roofs

Will a solar roof lower my electric bill?
Generating your own power reduces how much electricity you buy from the utility, which can lower monthly bills. Exact savings depend on your energy use, the system size, and your roof's sun exposure — a consultation sizes it to your home.
What's the difference between solar shingles and solar panels?
Solar shingles (like Timberline Solar™) are the roof — they're nailed on like asphalt shingles, so solar and roofing are one product. Integrated panels are best-in-class solar panels installed low-profile with a new shingle roof. Both avoid the bolted-on racks of traditional systems.
Will solar cause my roof to leak?
Roof-integrated solar is designed to prevent that. Solar shingles are nailed in like regular shingles, and integrated panels are flashed into the roof rather than bolted through it — so there are no rack penetrations to spring a leak.
Are there tax credits or incentives?
Powering your home with renewable energy can make you eligible for special incentives or tax credits. Programs change over time and depend on your situation, so confirm current details with a tax professional before you buy.
Can I see how much power it's making?
Yes. An integrated solar roof system includes a companion app that shows your production clearly — you can track energy by the hour, day, week, month, or year.
