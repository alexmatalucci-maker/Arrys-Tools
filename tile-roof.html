Extremely durable

Clay and concrete tile resist harsh weather, wind, insects, and rot. Their hard-wearing nature lets them last more than 50 years when constructed properly.

FACT: Individual tiles can be replaced without redoing the whole roof.

Easy to maintain

Because both materials are so tough and resistant to damage, it's unlikely you'll need extensive roof repairs down the road.

FACT: Routine care is mostly keeping valleys and gutters clear.

Naturally energy-smart

Tile's thermal mass and the air space beneath each tile create natural ventilation, helping keep a home cooler in the Florida heat.

FACT: That built-in airflow can ease the load on your A/C.

Genuinely stylish

Concrete comes in several profiles to complement any architecture; clay carries the timeless Spanish and Mediterranean look.

FACT: Tile is one of the biggest curb-appeal upgrades a home can make.

Common questions about tile roofs

How long does a tile roof last?
When properly constructed, clay and concrete tile roofs commonly last more than 50 years — and clay in particular can last a century or more. It's often the last roof a home will ever need. The underlayment beneath the tile is usually what's serviced over that span, not the tile itself.
What's the difference between clay and concrete tile?
Clay is fired from natural clay, colorfast through-and-through, and carries the classic Spanish/Mediterranean look with the longest lifespan. Concrete is strong, more affordable, and comes in more profiles and colors. Both are extremely durable and fire-resistant.
Is a tile roof too heavy for my house?
Tile is heavier than shingle or metal, so the home's structure needs to support it. Many Florida homes are already built for tile; if yours isn't, a roofing professional will evaluate the framing and advise whether reinforcement is needed before installation.
How does tile hold up in hurricanes?
Properly installed and fastened tile performs well against high wind, and its weight and Class A fire rating are real advantages. If a flying object cracks a tile, that single tile can be replaced without redoing the roof — one of tile's biggest long-term benefits.
Does tile fade over time?
Clay tile is colorfast because the color runs through the whole tile. Concrete tile can lighten slightly over many years of sun exposure, though modern color-bonded finishes hold up well. Both keep their character for decades.
