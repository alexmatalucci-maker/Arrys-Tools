Lifespan

A well-installed, well-ventilated architectural roof commonly lasts 25–30 years in Florida; a 3-Tab roof, roughly 15–20. Ventilation and sun exposure move the number most.

Wind & impact

Architectural shingles with a sealed nailing zone perform up to 130 mph installed to spec. Some lines add Class 3–4 impact resistance for hail and debris.

Warranties

Manufacturer warranties can run up to lifetime on materials, with separate wind and algae coverage — and stronger workmanship coverage when a certified contractor registers it.

Maintenance

Low, not zero: keep valleys and gutters clear, check after major storms, and address lifted or missing shingles early to protect the deck underneath.

Common questions about shingles

How long does a shingle roof last in Florida?
A well-installed, well-ventilated architectural shingle roof typically lasts 25–30 years here; a 3-Tab roof, roughly 15–20. Sun exposure, attic ventilation, and maintenance are the biggest factors — and proper installation matters more than any single one.
What's the difference between 3-Tab and architectural shingles?
3-Tab shingles are a single flat layer — uniform and the most affordable. Architectural shingles are multi-layered, so they're thicker, cast a dimensional shadow line, last longer, and carry higher wind ratings. Architectural is the most common choice today.
Can a shingle roof handle hurricane-force wind?
Installed correctly, modern architectural shingles are rated up to 130 mph. Reaching that rating depends on the full system — starter strips, the proper nailing pattern, sealed ridge caps, and roof-deck protection all work together.
Why do shingle roofs get black streaks?
That's blue-green algae, which Florida humidity feeds. Shingles with algae-resistant granules (backed by a 25-year warranty on premium lines) resist that discoloration and hold their color far longer.
How much maintenance does a shingle roof need?
Not much — keep gutters and valleys clear of debris, do a visual check after major storms, and repair any lifted, cracked, or missing shingles promptly so water can't reach the decking below.
